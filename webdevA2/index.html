Biology of turtles

Turtles are an order of reptiles characterised by the special shell developed mainly from their ribs. They are found on most continents, some islands and much of the ocean in the case of sea turtles. There are approximately 360 living and recently extinct species of turtle, including land-dwelling tortoises and freshwater terrapins.

Shell
The shell helps protect the soft body of the turtle from predators and provide shelter
Head
Turtles have very flexible necks, and can retract their heads into their shells when threatened
Leg
Turtles have 4 legs that can also be retracted into the shell, various species have different adaptations in their legs to make them more suited to their environment.

Types of Turtles

Tortoises and terrapins are subspecies of turtle. With tortoises having evolved to survive on land and terrapins adapting to freshwater environments. Below are a few species of turtle and some information about them.

Sea Turtles

Green Sea Turtle
Adult Length: ~1.5m
Lifespan: ~90 yrs
Found: Shallow Lagoons

Hawksbill Sea Turtle
Adult Length: ~1m
Lifespan: 50-60 yrs
Found: Lagoons & Coral Reefs

Tortoises

Elongated Tortoise
Adult Length: ~30cm
Lifespan: 40-50 yrs
Found: Southeast Asia

Indian Star Tortoise
Adult Length: ~30cm
Lifespan: 35-80 yrs
Found: India

Terrapins

Batagur Baska
Adult Length: ~60cm
Lifespan: 40-50 yrs
Found: Rivers in South Asia

Red-eared Slider
Adult Length: ~30cm
Lifespan: 30 yrs
Found: Globally (Invasive)
